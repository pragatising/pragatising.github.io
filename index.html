about



1991

Born! Two important things followed - I started painting, sketching early in my life. My father would hate it so much, he would lock my art supplies in the Godrej (Godrej is an Indian thing. It’s an almirah and Godrej is the brand but Godrej became synonymous to Almirah).

Sorry I digressed! I thought I can be an artist, life happened, middle class Indian family, I ended up doing engineering because “financial stability”.

2010

Started my undergrad in Electrical Engineering.


For four years, in an engineering college did everything except engineering - photography, graphic design, digital art (alternative routes to design)and what not! Despite that, I still didn’t think of pursuing a creative career. How could I? In the Maslow’s pyramid, my family and I were at the bottom.

2014

Graduated with 6.7 GPA out of 10!


First job as a Programmer Analyst/UI Developer at Cognizant.

2015

Switched to PwC (PwC was a better brand name compared to Cognizant! Sarcasm!!)


It was 2016, I was in Bombay where I met my sister’s ex-boyfriend - Utkarsh, one of my kind, an engineer turned Designer. I was blown by his creativity, humility and all the knowledge he had to share. Right now, he is finishing his MS in Visual Communication at Alto University, Helsinki.

A lot of credit goes to him for exposing me to this amazing world! He introduced me (an engineer) Don Norman, Bruno Munari, Swiss Design, Thinking with Type, Marshall McLuhan. And now I was here, to stay!

(You might be thinking “Why didn’t she just google about design! Well, I grew up without internet till 2009. My first computer was in 2010, gifted to me by my Aunt! I didn’t know what I didn’t know, and hence barely could have googled!)


Meanwhile, first year at PwC, I mostly did research, but I really wanted to do UI/UX design. I found an opportunity and there was no letting it go. My first project was designing a land records management system for a Cement Manufacturing Client. The dashboard is currently in use in 40 plants in India and has reduced the records processing time to 1 day compared to 2 weeks.

2017

By this time, I knew what I wanted. Coming from engineering backrground, I really wanted to hone my Visual Design skills. I wanted to be a T-shaped designer and be able to wear different hats as an when required.


I had enough savings by now, so I quit my job and spent some time doing Visual Design for a year. I did a lot of digital illustrations, got a couple of UI/UX projects.

2018



Three applications, one admit, and 2018 August I flew to Atlanta, to pursue my MS in HCI at Georgia Tech.



I guess, apart from UX Design, I really really wanted to study for the first time not for job or stability, but to actually study. Apart from skill buidlding, took theory heavy classes such as Design Philosophy, Feminist Theory, Critical Theory, Experimental Media, Social Informatics.


2020

Graduated amidst global pandemic!!!!!!!!



What next?
