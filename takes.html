Un-interrupted Note-taking


Product Design | Aug'18-Dec'18
Manasee Narvilkar and Pragati Singh



Overview



The inspiration for this project came from personal experience of reading books and academic papers. Many serious book readers were facing a problem when trying to reflect on their readings or finding notes that they had taken earlier. Once I was having a discussion with my friend who is an avid reader and he wanted to share something he recently read, although he had taken a photo of that page but he could not find that photo as it got buried somewhere. He kept scrolling through his gallery to find that photo. This incident have us idea to re-imagine the process of reading, taking notes and sharing in digital times.



Research Question

How might we improve the experience of reading and note-taking for serious academic readers?



Design Goals

Help the user extract content without interrupting the flow of reading for later perusal and meaning-making.



My Role

We were a two member team, hence from interviews, observations, literature reviews to visual design we both worked upon pretty much all aspects of the solution.



Project Timeline





Our Solution

We designed a mobile that helps readers take photo of the excerpts and simultaneously extract the text from the images. Readers can then save the note and organize them by book name, author name etc. Readers can readily share these notes on social media.







Photo based note-taking and instant sharing


  • Take photo using inbuilt camera function. Instantaneously extract text for better readability.

  • Organize with context such as book name and author.

  • Share with friends and followers on social media.

Prototype


The high-fidelity prototype was made in Adobe XD. The app allows academics and readers to organise their notes in the form of images and texts along with context. It maps with the book structure and how serious academic readers like to retrieve notes while having conversations.

Design Features



Notes Taxonomy
Based on our interviews, we found that our readers were having hard time finding their notes as they were highly unorganized. Hence we designed a taxonomy to organize the transcribed notes by capturing the meta-data of their readings. The app has both list view and grid view and allows retrieval based on book name, author, etc.

Flexibility
Readers can perform various functions on these notes, e.g., delete, edit, share, copy, paste. ​ Synthesize information A lot of times, when our readers try to understand their old notes, it was difficult for them to synthesise the meaning and the GIST of their own notes. The app has a mind map to visualize the GIST of their notes. They can click on the bubbles to filter their notes by the GIST that these bubbles encapsulate.

​ Preserve emotional aspect
One of the crucial objectives of our design was to organize the notes not just with the context but also preserve their emotional aspect. The mobile app would present the notes along with its context which includes author, book, time etc. The notes are organised in the form of cards.

​ Sharing
During our interview we found that our readers wanted to share their notes with other people as part of their discussion or debate. The app would allow them to share their notes with their friends and family.
