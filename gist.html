Un-interrupted Note-taking


Product Design | Aug'18-Dec'18
Manasee Narvilkar and Pragati Singh



Overview

The idea of this project came from personal experience of reading books and academic papers. Many serious book readers were facing a problem when trying to reflect on their readings or finding notes that they had taken earlier. We wanted to re-imagine the process of reading and taking notes.



My Role

We were a two member team, hence from interviews, observations, literature reviews to visual design we both worked upon pretty much all aspects of the solution.



Research Question

How might we improve the experience of reading and note-taking for serious academic readers?



Design Goals

Help the user extract content without interrupting the flow of reading for later perusal and meaning-making.



Project Timeline





Our Solution

An auditory interface that helps readers record their notes and organises them with their context and other relevant metadata.

Design Features



Notes Taxonomy
Based on our interviews, we found that our readers were having hard time finding their notes as they were highly unorganised. Hence we designed a taxonomy to organise the transcribed notes by capturing the metadata of their readings. The app has both list view and grid view and allows fast search based on tags/metadata. Based on the keywords analysed from transcription the app will auto-recognise the name of the book, author etc by referring to the internet.

Flexibility
Readers can perform various functions on these notes, e.g., delete, edit, share, copy, paste. ​ Synthesize information A lot of times, when our readers try to understand their old notes, it was difficult for them to synthesise the meaning and the GIST of their own notes. The app has a mind map to visualise the GIST of their notes. They can click on the bubbles to filter their notes by the GIST that these bubbles encapsulate.

​ Preserve emotional aspect
One of the crucial objectives of our design was to organise the notes not just with the context but also preserve their emotional aspect. The mobile app would present the notes along with its context which includes author, book, time etc. The notes are organised in the form of cards.

​ Sharing
During our interview we found that our readers wanted to share their notes with other people as part of their discussion or debate. The app would allow them to share their notes with their friends and family.
