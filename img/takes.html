Takes - Uninterrupted note-taking


Product Design | Aug'18-Dec'18
Manasee Narvilkar & Pragati Singh

Overview



The inspiration for this project came from personal experience of reading and sharing. Once I was having a discussion with my friend who is an avid reader and he wanted to share something he had recently read, although he had taken a photo of that page but he could not find that photo as it got buried deep in his photo gallery. He kept scrolling through his gallery to find that photo. This incident gave us idea to re-imagine the process of reading, taking photo-notes and sharing in digital times.

Research Question

How might we improve the experience of photo-note taking that allows easy organisation and quicker sharing?

Design Goals

Design seamless experience of taking, organising and sharing notes on the go.

My Role

Project Timeline

Our Solution

We designed a mobile that helps readers take photo of the excerpts and simultaneously extract the text from the images. Readers can then save the note and organize them by book name, author name etc. Readers can readily share these notes on social media.





Photo based note-taking and instant sharing


  • Take photo using inbuilt camera function. Instantaneously extract text for better readability.

  • Organize with context such as book name and author.

  • Share with friends and followers on social media.

Prototype


The high-fidelity prototype was made in Adobe XD. The app allows academics and readers to organise their notes with context and support multimodal retrieval/search. It maps with the book structure and how serious academic readers like to retrieve notes while having conversations.

Interviews

We interviewed 4 users,both of us interviewed two users each. Two of the users were PhD students while the other two were avid users working at different places. A general theme was though all of our users were either into humanities or interested in it. We conducted these interviews over two days. The interviews were semi-structured and in some two of the cases, users showed us their note-taking process as well. One of the interview was though done over phone call.





Storyboarding the emerging user-story



After the interview, we created a storyboard to understand the journey of our users while reading and taking notes. This helped us have a coherent view of the process and the sub-tasks users tried to accomplish. On the basis of the storyboard we created persona of two user groups that we wanted to focus on.

Design & Evaluation



Our design process included sketching ideas,body-storming, then getting feedback and reiterating on the deign to develop hi-fidelity prototype and usability testing. We designed the system flow and sketched a number of interventions/concepts.

















Interactive Prototype - Iteration 1

This is the first iteration of the interactive prototype. We used icons to show books and notes. We made a conscious choice of relying on texts rather graphics, for reading purposes, we imagined the visual language to be more typographic than illustrated.











Evaluation and Iteration - 2

Once we had our first hi-fidelity prototype, we conducted cognitive walkthrough with our interactive prototype, to evaluate our application. We conducted the evaluation with 5 people over 2 days and came up with the following findings :

  • The layout on authors screen was confusing for users. They didn't quite understand if the small boxes with the name of the books were clickable. Usual expectaion was to see some note.
  • Common feedback was to provide with upfront sharing rather open the whole post and share.
  • The book icons and notes icon were not differentiable
  • Number of notes saved under each book would give a good overview/ insight of the reading pattern.
  • The top navigation was conflicting with conventional behaviour of bottom navigation

With the above feedback, I iterated on the prototype to have the following final version. In this iteration we also worked on designing the icons for the nav bar.
